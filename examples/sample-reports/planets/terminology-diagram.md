════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
⚠  THE CONFLICT THAT MATTERS MOST
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Capitalization is the only signal separating generic "moon" (any natural satellite) from proper-noun "Moon" (Earth's), and dwarf-planets.md also uses "satellite" for the same concept when describing Charon — a reader skimming, or any case-insensitive search/TMS extraction, could conflate Earth's Moon with a generic exomoon reference.

THE BEST EXISTING PRACTICE
dwarf-planets.md is the most precise and internally consistent source: it expands "International Astronomical Union (IAU)" on first use before switching to the acronym, and applies its dwarf-planet definition uniformly across every section.
